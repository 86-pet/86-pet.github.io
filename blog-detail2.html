How to care for cat

With a mischievous nature, purring gestures and a cute face, cats are ideal animals for pets. However, contrary to popular belief, cats are not pets that you can let them "take care of"! If you want your cat to live healthy and happy, you need to know how to care for your cat and give your four-legged friend the best life possible from the moment you get home.

Method 1: Teach your cat toilet

Encourage your cat to use litter trays: Thanks to the texture of the litter box, most cats will prefer the litter tray to other parts of the house. However, there are still a few steps you need to take to get your cat to want to poop in the litter box.

  • Place the litter tray in a quiet place where the cat will not be disturbed by people or dogs passing by or making lots of noise.
  • To keep the sand tray clean, be sure to scoop out dirty sand daily and clean it weekly. You should also change the sand at least once a week.
  • If you have more than one cat, provide enough litter box for them. Assuming there are 2 cats in the house, you need 3 sand trays placed in many parts of the house. An authoritative cat can prevent the weaker one from using certain litter boxes.

Method 2: Feed the cat

Decide what type of food you want to eat. Cat food comes in a multitude of varieties: dry food, wet food, and canned food are common types. Dry food is easier to store, but cats love the taste of wet and canned foods. These two types also help add more fluids to your cat's diet. In general, what kind of food is the owner's preference for the cat.

Cats sometimes need to eat one type of food over another due to a medical condition. Consult with your veterinarian about this.

Method 3: Keep the cat’s healthy

Grooming cats depending on their needs. You may think that cats don't need someone to groom them because they can groom themselves. However, you really need to brush short-haired cats weekly and long-haired cats a few times a week. This will help reduce hair loss in the home and prevent puffiness in your cat's stomach.

With cats often molt (especially long-haired cats), you should use small tooth metal comb to brush into coat underneath and remove hair fall out.
