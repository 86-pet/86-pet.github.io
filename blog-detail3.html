Tips and warnings when breeding guinea

Never bath your hamster with regular soap, as it can dry out your hamster's skin. Go to a pet store to buy hamster soap.

Advice:

Never bath your hamster with regular soap, as it can dry out your hamster's skin. Go to a pet store to buy hamster soap.

Some guinea may gnaw you to explore. They will stop when they realize you are not food.

Use a heavy bowl to feed your hamster. They can chew on plastic bowls or topple the bowl lightly.

Do not put your hamster on your bed or place it on high surfaces. They can fall down and hurt. You should also be careful not to drop your hamster.

To train your hamster to go to the bathroom, you can spread a little shredded paper in the corner of the cage.

Your hamster will bite when startled, so be careful and gentle when handling it in your hand.

To let your hamster exercise every day, release it on the safe, fenced lawn. Hamsters love to run.

Always check your hamster morning and afternoon.

It will take your hamster 3 - 5 days to get to know you and their new home. Don't be frustrated if they don't come to you the first day you bring them back.

Hamsters eat everything, so make sure they don't have access to paper, books, etc.

If you only keep one hamster for a long time, don’t keep another one. A hamster can become aggressive if it is used to being alone,.

Warning:

You need to be very careful when buying your hamster at the pet store to make sure it doesn't get sick. Check around the hamster's nose, eyes, and ears.

Hamsters usually hide their symptoms until they become very sick. Once there are signs of illness, guinea pigs will get worse, even die very quickly. You should talk to your doctor as soon as possible.

If you feed your hamster grass, make sure it is free of chemicals. And the fresher the grass, the better!

Your guinea pig runs very quickly and may overtake you if it gets out of the cage, so be careful! Make sure the "playing field" is enclosed so that they do not escape or fall into dangerous situations.

Avoid using commercially available toys and rewards. Many products are not good for your hamster, so replace them with healthier options like fruits or vegetables, 1-2 times a week in small amounts.

Do not give hamster US lettuce, potatoes, onions, leeks, tomato leaves, chocolate, corn, wheat, dairy products, meat, mushrooms, fast food, or alcoholic beverages.

Never use an exercise ball or hamster wheel. Even when labeled for a guinea pig, these items are not safe and could damage the hamster's legs, nails, and spine.

If your hamster has ringworm around its eyes or nose, it may have an inflammation of the upper respiratory tract and should be promptly referred to a veterinarian for treatment.

Be alert to the advice of pet store staff. Very few of them have the training and understanding of what is best for a guinea pig.

Always clean the cage every 3 days if you use straw to line the bottom of the cage, as maggots can multiply in the straw. Make sure the straw is not too stiff, as the sharp straws can damage your hamster's eyes.

Never leave your hamster unattended overhead. They can fall and break their legs.

Never place your hamster's cage in direct sunlight outdoors.
