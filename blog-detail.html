Deworming your dog and what you need to know

Dog worm removal and worm prevention are very important. Just like humans, dogs are also prone to diseases caused by helminths. Can you know when to deworming for dogs and dog deworming drugs safe? But many owners assume that deworming is done, and their dog will be safe. Have you noticed if your dog refuses to take the medication or the side effects he experiences after deworming? Don't worry too much, the 86Pet article below will help you.

The age of use and the dog worming schedule

Worming puppies need to be done in a serious and procedural manner. The best time is when puppies are 2-3 weeks old. Worms are dangerous parasites. They multiply and develop into the environment very quickly. Bleaching during this time will prevent the eggs from spreading out.

Because your puppy's body and resistance are not stable, you need to observe them closely. If there are abnormal symptoms, call your veterinarian immediately for advice. Worm remover in the dog again in weeks 4, 6 and 8.

How to properly deworming dogs

Dogs don't like to take drugs, and dog worming is no exception. They always close their mouths and resist fiercely. Some dogs burp back up. In both cases, dogs received drugs in very small doses.

The easiest way is to put the pills in their food. Since dogs often gnaw and swallow food, the medication will be easy to take. There are tricky dogs that will recognize the medicine in their food. For these puppies you will have to put the medication directly in their mouth. Or can be crushed and mixed with their food. Maybe your puppy won't find out and you will be successful.

Depending on the breed ... there will be different doses. You should pay close attention to this issue. Avoid too much drug shock. A dog deworming pill is not too expensive and how much it costs. Be sure to keep a regular schedule for deworming your puppies.
